How to Use the Weighted Grade Calculator

Enter each grade and its corresponding weight percentage. The calculator automatically computes your weighted average grade.

What Is a Weighted Grade?

A weighted grade assigns different levels of importance to assignments, projects, quizzes and exams. Higher-weighted assessments contribute more to the final course grade.

Weighted Grade Formula

Weighted Grade = (Grade × Weight) + (Grade × Weight) + ...

Example Calculation

Suppose:

  • Assignment Grade: 90 (40%)
  • Final Exam Grade: 80 (60%)

Weighted Grade = (90 × 0.40) + (80 × 0.60)

Weighted Grade = 36 + 48 = 84

Your weighted course grade would be 84%.

Why Weighted Grades Matter

Many schools, colleges and universities use weighted grading systems. Understanding weighted averages helps students track performance and determine how important future assignments and exams will be.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a weighted average?

A weighted average gives more importance to certain grades based on their percentage contribution to the final grade.

Do the weights need to total 100%?

Yes. For the most accurate result, all assignment and exam weights should add up to 100%.

Can I use this calculator for college courses?

Yes. It works for high school, college and university grading systems.
